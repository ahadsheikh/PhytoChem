The premier phytochemical database, phytochemdb.com, represents an authentic repository site for plant-derived phytochemicals that can ameliorate Computer-Aided Drug Design, Lead Optimization, Virtual Screening and Molecular Docking as well as in the bioinformatics sector.
Currently, {{ n_plants }} plants from the Asian territory and their corresponding {{ n_compounds }} phytochemicals are available in this database that will escalate promptly.
Three dimensional (3D) structures in tandem with Canonical SMILES are incorporated in the database with the intention of facile retrieval of phytochemical-related information. This sumptuous database assembles the physicochemical properties inclusive of Molecular Formula, Molecular Weight, No. of heavy atoms, No. of aromatic heavy atoms, Fraction Csp3, No. of rotatable bonds, No. of H-bond acceptors, No. H-bond donors, Molar Refractivity, and TPSA (Topological polar surface area). Furthermore, the assessment of pharmacokinetics properties, including GI absorption, BBB permeant, P-gp substrate, CYP1A2 inhibitor, CYP2C19 inhibitor, CYP2C9 inhibitor, CYP2D6 inhibitor, CYP3A4 inhibitor, and Log Kp (skin permeation), is available in that immense database. Additionally, drug-likeness prominences-oriented assessment, such as Lipinski rule of five, Ghose, Veber, Egan, Muegge, and Bioavailability Score, is assembled in the database. Supplementarily, the massive database congregates medicinal chemistry-based features inclusive of PAINS, Brenk, Leadlikeness, and Synthetic accessibility in conjunction with lipophilicity properties such as iLogP.
Anyone can inquiry about phytochemicals by the unique accession number, Canonical SMILES, Molecular formula and plant names as well. Phytochemicals obtained from each plant can be found all together by simply searching the name of the plant.
This plant-derived phytochemical database will be immensely efficient for the researchers, professors, and learners of bioinformatics who are inquisitive at Computer-Aided Drug Design, Lead Optimization, Virtual Screening and Molecular Docking.
Researchers who have screened phytochemicals in their laboratory and already published their data can contribute to this database. They can upload the three-dimensional structure of the phytochemicals in this database and the new uploaded compound will get a unique accession number after passing the review procedure.
